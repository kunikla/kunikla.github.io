Energy Action Network website

Genesis

The former EAN WordPress website was 7 years old, and was becoming increasingly difficult to maintain. It relied on an orphaned theme, and several orphaned plugins, one of which would not display its output on Chrome. Over the years, content had been added without restructuring the menus or Post Categories, so it was difficult to find content, or to decide where to add new content.

Development

The primary goal was to update the underlying technology. The effort started with a modern theme with a modern look and more features, especially more widget areas. The content displayed in each widget area was customizable on a per-page basis. Only well-supported plugins were installed. The site was moved to a hosting plan with better servers and support, an SSL certificate was added, and the site uses the CloudFlare CDN to deliver files. Daily off-site backups are performed, Google Analytics gives staff needed feedback, and the site has appropriate security features installed.

Another goal was to re-organize the content to highlight the most recent activities of the organization, and to prune out-of-date content (pages, posts, and media) that was no longer useful.

A final goal was to give EAN a website with a design that was similar to the design of its Annual Reports. The colors and font were set to match the 2017 Report, and many graphics from the report were used to tell the organization's story.
