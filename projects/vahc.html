VAHC Website

Genesis

This website was developed under the auspices of Code for BTV, a brigade of Code for America, for the benefit of the Vermont Affordable Housing Coalition.

Purpose

The previous VAHC website was custom-built in 2007. The layout, features, and content on most pages could only be changed by modifying the PHP code. New content of limited types could be entered via an administrative interface. That interface could only be changed by modifying the PHP code. A new feature, "News", had been added in 2012 by creating a Wordpress site in a subdirectory, styling its theme to match the look and feel of the custom website, and updating the code in the Menu module to add an entry. A new WordPress site had been started by a volunteer in 2016, with some work having been done on styling and updated content, but the project had been abandoned.

Project Story

On August 25, 2018, the 2018 National Day of Civic Hacking, Code for BTV held an event to address the tech needs of several agencies that work to provide affordable housing in Vermont. VAHC had 2 needs: a new website, and re-organization of its contacts. The contacts were gathered, and re-organization begun, but that effort has been put on hiatus until a volunteer can be found to complete the task. I migrated the abandoned Wordpress site to a staging site, and discussion with Coordinator Erhard Mahnke about how to proceed began that day. Others present at the event helped to inventory the old site, and put the preliminary requirements for the new website in writing.

In September, Kenda Tucker was hired as a VISTA Communications & Outreach Coordinator, and she began updating, creating, and re-organizing content. Two additional meetings were held at VAHC offices to incorporate additional feedback from staff and the Steering Committee, but most communication occurred using email and Slack. In November, the free theme was replaced with an Accessibility Ready paid theme with many more options for styling, which Kenda promptly took advantage of. The site was deployed on December 15 at the VAHC offices. The process took about three hours, with some follow-up work that evening and over the next week.

Improvements made possible by creating a new site:
  • The layout and styling make the site more readable.
  • A slideshow on the home page creates a more modern and inviting first impression.
  • A more flexible and robust menu system allows for better organization of content.
  • The site is accessible to those with disabilities.
  • The client can update content on all pages.
  • A better editor in the back end makes the job of updating the site's content easier.
  • Through the availability of widget areas, updating the header, footer, and sidebar is easier. These can even be customized on a page-by-page basis.
  • The site is now regularly backed up and updated, enhancing security and reliability.
  • Should additional features be identified as desirable, it will be easier to add them.
